Methodology

How Horizon Ledger builds, scores, and maintains its indexes.

Overview

Horizon Ledger is a local investment research system that uses publicly available data — SEC EDGAR filings, Yahoo Finance prices, and Federal Reserve (FRED) macro indicators — to build systematic factor models. No proprietary data, no paid feeds.

Two public-facing indexes are maintained: a Conservative Index targeting quality, value, and income; and an Aggressive Index targeting momentum and growth.

Data Sources

  • Prices & market cap: Yahoo Finance (yfinance) — adjusted close prices, 5-year history
  • Fundamentals: SEC EDGAR XBRL bulk data — revenue, earnings, assets, debt, dividends, shares outstanding. A mandatory 45-day lag from fiscal quarter-end is applied before any fundamental is used, to prevent look-ahead bias.
  • Macro indicators: FRED API — 10-year Treasury yield, 2-year yield, VIX, HY credit spread, CPI, Sahm Rule, TIPS breakeven inflation
  • Shiller CAPE: Yale CAPE monthly data for long-term valuation context

Universe Construction

The stock universe starts with S&P 500 constituents and expands to include stocks meeting these minimum criteria:

  • Market cap ≥ $100 million
  • Average daily volume ≥ $1 million notional (20-day)
  • Listed for at least 365 days (survivorship bias control)

Delisted tickers are tracked and excluded from prospective scoring (no survivorship bias). Stocks with significant data gaps receive neutral (50th percentile) fills for missing factors rather than being dropped entirely.

Conservative Index — Factors

13 factors across five categories, sector-neutralized:

  • Quality (23%): Gross Profitability (Novy-Marx 2013), ROIC, Piotroski F-Score (Piotroski 2000), Altman Z-Score (normalized)
  • Value (25%): Earnings Yield (EBIT/EV), FCF Yield, EV/EBITDA (inverted)
  • Safety (8%): Debt-to-Equity (inverted), Current Ratio
  • Low Volatility (12%): 252-day realized volatility (inverted) — Blitz & van Vliet (2007)
  • Income (15%): Dividend Yield, 5-year Dividend Growth, Consecutive Increase Streak

Hard exclusions: Altman Z < 1.81 (distress zone), annualized volatility > 45%, market cap < $5B.

Position sizing: Half-Kelly optimization — w_i ∝ 0.5 × (score_i / volatility_i²). Achieves approximately 75% of optimal growth at 50% of Kelly volatility.

Aggressive Index — Factors

11 factors across four categories, sector-neutralized:

  • Momentum (35%): 12-month price momentum (skip-1-month, Jegadeesh & Titman 1993), 6-month momentum, sector momentum
  • Growth (30%): Revenue CAGR (5-year), Earnings CAGR (5-year), Revenue Trajectory
  • Quality (25%): Gross Profitability, ROIC, Piotroski F-Score
  • Technical (10%): RSI signal, Volume Confirmation

Hard exclusions: Altman Z < 1.81, revenue YoY decline > 20%.

Position sizing: Equal weight (1/N) — simpler, avoids estimation error in high-turnover strategies.

Scoring Process

  1. Raw factor computation — point-in-time fundamentals and prices for each stock
  2. Cross-sectional percentile rank — each factor ranked 0–100 across all universe stocks
  3. Sector neutralization — z-score within GICS sector, then re-rank cross-sectionally. Prevents hidden sector tilts.
  4. Weighted composite score — factor percentiles combined by strategy weights
  5. Risk overlay — hard exclusions applied (Altman Z, vol, revenue decline)
  6. Rank and select top 25

Rebalancing

  • Conservative: Quarterly (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct). Buffer zone: stocks exit only when rank falls below 35th position (40% buffer) to reduce turnover.
  • Aggressive: Monthly. Tighter buffer (rank > 35) given higher expected turnover in momentum strategies.
  • 5% drift threshold triggers a rebalance flag between scheduled dates.

Market Regime Detection

A Hidden Markov Model (HMM) classifies the market into three regimes — Bull, Neutral, and Bear — using VIX, yield curve, price momentum, and breadth signals. The regime does not directly modify factor weights but provides context for the Market Pulse and newsletter commentary.

Information Coefficient Tracking

After 60+ weekly predictions accumulate, the system computes Spearman rank correlation (IC) between each factor's score and realized 63-day forward returns. Factors with IC < 0.01 are flagged for review. IC Information Ratio (mean IC / std IC) above 0.5 indicates consistent predictive power. This is used to validate and optionally adjust factor weights over time.

What This Is Not

  • Not a live brokerage or trading system — all portfolios are paper/simulated
  • Not backtested to prove profitability — historical performance is live tracking from the start date, not a backtest
  • Not optimized to show good numbers — factor weights are set from academic literature and fixed until IC tracking provides sufficient evidence to update them
  • Not financial advice of any kind
